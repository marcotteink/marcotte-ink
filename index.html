About Matt Marcotte

I'm an operational strategist with 15+ years in the screen-printing and DTF industry — on the press, in the art room, and running operations. I've helped shops generating up to $30 million a year scale, leading teams of 100+ and building the SOPs and systems that keep them efficient and profitable.

I also build the software printers use: DTF.STREAM for transfers, ScreenPrintGPT for AI-powered shop help, and Free Gang Sheet Maker to cut wasted film. And I write about the craft (PrintHustlers, Printavo, and this blog), because the best shops share what works.

FAQ — fig.06

Frequently asked

Who is Matt Marcotte?

Matt Marcotte is a screen-printing and DTF (direct-to-film) industry veteran and operations strategist. He helps print shops run leaner and grow — he has scaled shops to $30 million a year and led teams of 100+ — and he is the founder of DTF.STREAM and ScreenPrintGPT and the creator of Free Gang Sheet Maker.

What does Matt do for print shops?

Operations consulting: building SOPs and systems, integrating software, improving efficiency and profitability, and helping owners scale sustainably and predictably. The goal is a shop that runs leaner and grows on purpose.

What size shops does Matt work with?

From owner-operators doing six figures up to shops generating $30 million a year. The work is tailored to where your shop is now and where you want to take it.

What software has Matt built?

DTF.STREAM (DTF transfer printing and gang sheets), ScreenPrintGPT (an AI assistant trained for print shops), and Free Gang Sheet Maker (a free, browser-based DTF gang-sheet tool).

Can Matt help with data, automation, or AI?

Yes. Matt builds custom integrations, automated reporting dashboards, and AI workflows that get print shops better data — faster and cleaner. He connects Printavo and other shop software to QuickBooks, Google Sheets, Slack, and Zapier, builds production and sales dashboards, and creates AI tools trained for print shops. See print shop data & AI →

Does Matt write about screen printing?

Yes — his blog covers halftones, ink, hoodie printing, color separation, shop operations, and interviews with the industry, and he has contributed to PrintHustlers and Printavo.

How do I work with Matt?

Email matt@marcotte.ink with a bit about your shop and what you want to improve, and he'll take it from there.
